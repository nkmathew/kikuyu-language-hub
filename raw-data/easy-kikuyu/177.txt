You need to write sentences in Kikuyu stating what the people listed in numbers 1 to 6 do, using the specified verbs. The example structure is: **(Pronoun) arutaga wīra wa (verb)**.

Here are the sentences, following the structure and using the correct verb and subject for each number:

1.  **(Nii) Ndī mūhūri wa mbica** (I am a photographer) $\rightarrow$ **Ndīrutaga wīra wa gūhūra mbica** (I work to take photos)
2.  **(Wee) wī mūgŵīmi** (You are a hunter) $\rightarrow$ **Ūrutaga wīra wa kūgŵīma** (You work to hunt)
3.  **(We) nī mūriīthia wa mahiū** (He/she is a herder) $\rightarrow$ **Arutaga wīra wa kūrīīthia** (He/she works to herd)
4.  **(Inyuĩ) mwĩ aturi** (You are iron mongers) $\rightarrow$ **Mūrūtaga wīra wa gūtura** (You work to forge/smelt)
5.  **(O) nĩ ombi a nyũngũ** (They are potters) $\rightarrow$ **Marutaga wīra wa kūūmba** (They work to mold/make pots)
6.  **(Ūyū) nī mūgo** (This is a medicine person) $\rightarrow$ **Arutaga wīra wa kūragūra** (He/she works to heal/practice divination)

***

**Key Grammar Note:**

* The verb structure used is **(Pronoun prefix)-rutaga wīra wa (Infinitive Verb)**, which means "(Pronoun) works to (Verb)".
    * **Ndīrutaga** (I work) - from **Nii** (I)
    * **Ūrutaga** (You work) - from **Wee** (You singular)
    * **Arutaga** (He/She works) - from **We** (He/She) or **Ūyū** (This person)
    * **Mūrūtaga** (You plural work) - from **Inyuĩ** (You plural)
    * **Marutaga** (They work) - from **O** (They)

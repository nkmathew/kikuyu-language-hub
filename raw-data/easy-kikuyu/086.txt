The monosyllable word “O” is used to modify words and phrases in Kikuyu.
Here are some examples:
O (them; they)
O yothe (any)
O kûrîa maikagia maitho (Wherever they looked)
O rîrîa (when)
O hîndî o yothe (any time)
Mûndû o wothe (any person; every person)
O na kûrî o ûgûo (however; nevertheless)
Kî'ande o kîrîa (the same shoulder)
O na ndangîetîkîrîre (He/she would even not have agreed)
O ta mûndû (just like a person)
O rîmwe (suddenly; just once)
O na ndirarîrîkana (I don't even remember)

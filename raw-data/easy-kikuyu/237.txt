This image provides examples of **simple sentences** in Kĩkũyũ, specifically demonstrating the sounds of the consonants **G, H, and K**.

## Kĩkũyũ Simple Sentences: Consonants G, H, and K

### G (g)

| Kĩkũyũ Phrase | English Translation | Vocabulary Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gũka gũcia guoko gwaakwa** | Grandfather, pull my hand | **guoko** - hand |
| **Gĩra gĩkombe gĩkĩ** | Come for this cup | **Gĩkombe**  - cup |

---

### H (h)

| Kĩkũyũ Phrase | English Translation | Vocabulary Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Haha hena heho** | This place is cold | **Haha** - here |
| **Hũha mwaki wahota** | Blow the fire if you can | **Hũha** - blow; **mwaki** - fire |
| **Hoho nĩ ya hoha** | the capsicum has shriveled | **Hoho** - capsicum/pepper |

---

### K (k)

| Kĩkũyũ Phrase | English Translation | Vocabulary Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kaĩrĩtu kena karamu** | the girl has a pen | **Karamu**  - pen/pencil |
| **Kahĩĩ nĩ karatha kanyoni** | The boy has shot the bird | **Kahĩĩ** - boy; **kanyoni** - small bird |
| **Kana kona kora kora** | The child saw a frog and ran away | **Kiura**  - frog; **Kora** - small frog |

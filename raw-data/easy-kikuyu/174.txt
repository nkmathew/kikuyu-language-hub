### B. Conjugation: 3rd Person to 1st Person (Present/Near Future Tense)

This shows how verbs change from the 3rd person singular and plural (he/she/they) to the 1st person singular and plural (I/we). The example structure uses the **Nĩ a-** (3rd singular) and **Nĩ ma-** (3rd plural) prefixes.

| 3rd Person Singular (He/She) | 1st Person Singular (I) | 3rd Person Plural (They) | 1st Person Plural (We) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Nĩ aracaria** (is searching) | **Nĩ ndiracaria** (I am searching) | **Nĩ maroka** (are coming) | **Nĩ tũrĩoka** (We are coming) |
| **Nĩ aracoona** (is coming back) | **Nĩ ndiracooka** (I am coming back) | **Nĩ mararĩra** (are crying) | **Nĩ tũrĩarĩra** (We are crying) |
| **Nĩ areka** (is doing) | **Nĩ ndireka** (I am doing) | **Nĩ maraña** (are eating) | **Nĩ tũrĩara** (We are eating) |
| **Nĩ araikara** (is staying) | **Nĩ ndiraikara** (I am staying) | **Nĩ mararia** (are speaking) | **Nĩ tũrĩaria** (We are speaking) |
| **Nĩ arakinya** (is arriving) | **Nĩ ndirakinya** (I am arriving) | **Nĩ marakena** (are happy) | **Nĩ tũrĩakena** (We are happy) |
| **Nĩ arethamba** (is bathing) | **Nĩ ndirethamba** (I am bathing) | **Nĩ maratheka** (are laughing) | **Nĩ tũrĩatheka** (We are laughing) |
| **Nĩ arathũkia** (is going) | **Nĩ ndirathũkia** (I am going) | **Nĩ marathĩĩ** (are going) | **Nĩ tũrĩathĩĩ** (We are going) |
| **Nĩ aratinia** (is cutting) | **Nĩ ndiratinia** (I am cutting) | **Nĩ marathooma** (are reading) | **Nĩ tũrĩathooma** (We are reading) |
| **Nĩ areterera** (is waiting) | **Nĩ ndireterera** (I am waiting) | **Nĩ marandĩka** (are writing) | **Nĩ tũrĩandĩka** (We are writing) |

### C. Conjunctions and Connective Phrases

Most Kikuyu conjunctions are complete phrases, with a few single-word exceptions.

| English Conjunction | Kikuyu Word/Phrase | Example Sentences |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **and** | **na** | Nĩ marĩaga ngũrũwe **na** makanyua njohi (They eat pork **and** they drink beer). |
| **nor** | **na** | Matirĩaga ngũrũwe **na** matinyuaga njohi (They neither eat pork **nor** do they drink beer). |
| **but** | **no** | Nĩ mathakaga **no** matinaga (They play **but** they do not dance). |
| **or** | **kana** | No woye karamu **kana** ibuku (You can take a pen **or** a book). |
| **after** | **thutha wa...** | |
| **although** | **ona gũtũĩka...** | |
| **because** | **tondũ wa...** (niamu...) | |
| **till** | **kinya rĩrĩa** | |

### B. Short Story Excerpt (Karũgano)

The story **Wambũĩ Mĩcore na Thigiriri (Zebra and Black Ant)** describes the start of their friendship:

| Kikuyu Text | English Translation |
|:---|:---|
| **ciarĩ ũrata mũno** | Were great friends (had a **friendship**) |
| **Wambũĩ nĩ yatumaga rũgendo rũraihu** gũthiĩ **gũcererera Thigiriri** | Zebra would travel on a **long Journey** to **visit** Black Ant |
| No Thigiriri yarĩ **nini mũno** ya kũhota gũceragĩra Wambũĩ Mĩcore **kaĩngĩ** | But Black Ant was **very small** to be able to visit Zebra **often** |
| **Rĩrĩa** Thigiriri yaigwire atĩ... | **When** Black Ant heard that... |

Here is the transcription and translation of the Kikuyu story titled "Karũgano ka Wambũi Mĩcore na Njogu" (Story of Zebra and Elephant), presented in plain text:

***

## Transcription and Translation: Story of Zebra and Elephant

**Karũgano ka Wambũi Mĩcore na Njogu** - **Translation**
**Story of Zebra and Elephant**

* **Wambũi Mĩcore** - **Zebra**
* **yarĩ ĩmwe ya nyamũ** - **was one of the animals**
* **irĩa ciarĩ thaka mũno** - **that were most beautiful**
* **bũrũriinĩ wa nyamũ** - **in the country of animals**
* **Yarĩ na rũũa rũthaka** - **it had a beautiful hide (skin)**
* **rwa mĩkarara mĩerũ na mĩirũ** - **with black and white stripes**
* **Nyamũ ciothe** - **all the animals**
* **ciamĩroragĩra ĩgegete mũno** - **looked at (Zebra) in awe**
* **Nyamũ cionaga** - **the animals saw**
* **ĩrĩ ya mathekania mũno** - **as very funny**
* **Yatũmaga matheke** - **it made them laugh**
* **kinya makoma mbaru** - **until their ribs ached (hardened)**
* **Mamĩũragia atĩrĩrĩ** - **They used to ask it...**
* **Mĩcore, ũhũraga thũ cĩaku atĩa?** - **Striped one, how do you fight your enemies?**
* **Mĩcore yarũgamaga** - **The striped one would stand**
* **na magũrũ ma mbere** - **with the forelegs**
* **ĩgaikĩia mateke** - **and kicked**
* **na magũrũ ma thutha** - **with the rear legs**
* **Nyamũ ciothe** - **all the animals**
* **cĩagũagĩa na mũtheko** - **fell down laughing (broke into laughter)**
* **Hĩtĩ nĩyo yathekaga** - **hyena is the one that laughed**
* **na mũgambo mũnene** - **in a loud voice**
* **gũkĩra nyamũ irĩa ingĩ** - **than all the other animals**
* **O na ũmũthĩ** - **even today**
* **hĩtĩ yarĩrikana** - **when hyena remembers**
* **ũrĩa Wambũi Mĩcore** - **how the zebra**
* **ĩrũaga** - **fights**
* **ĩthekaga ĩrĩ ĩkĩ ũtuko** - **it laughs alone in the night**

(A faded watermark vertically on the right side reads: "Wĩrute Gĩkũyũ - Jĩfunze Kikuyu")

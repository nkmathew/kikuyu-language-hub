## Grammatical Notes on Class 4

The image highlights the key concords (or modifiers) used with Class 4 nouns:

| Concord Type | Singular (Kĩ-/Gĩ-) | Plural (Ci-/I-) | Example from image | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **This/These** (Demonstrative) | **Gĩkĩ** or **Kĩkĩ** | **Ici** or **Cici** | **Gĩtĩ gĩkĩ kimwe** | **This one chair** |
| **These** (Demonstrative) | | **Itĩ ici igĩrĩ** | **These two chairs** |
| **These** (Demonstrative, alternative) | **Ici** | | |

As noted in the image:
* The **Noun form** changes from **Kĩ-/Gĩ-** (singular) to **Ci-/I-** (plural).
* The demonstrative (**This/These**) and the numbers **-mwe** (one) and **-gĩrĩ** (two) change their prefixes to agree with the noun class (**Gĩkĩ** / **Kĩmwe** in singular and **Ici** / **Igĩrĩ** in plural).
* Other modifiers like adjectives (e.g., **-thũ** "bad") remain in their noun form and don't change to match the concord (e.g., **kĩrĩma kĩnene** "a big mountain" $\rightarrow$ **ciĩma nene** "big mountains").

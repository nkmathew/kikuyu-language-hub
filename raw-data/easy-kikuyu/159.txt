This image presents a Kikuyu **proverb** (**Thimo**) about child-rearing and provides a few example sentences.

-----

## Kikuyu Proverb and Vocabulary

| Kikuyu Proverb (Thimo) | Literal Translation | Underlying Meaning (Lesson) |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Mũtĩ ũrũngagwo ũrĩ mũnini** | **A tree is straightened when young (when still a sapling)** | **Do not wait till your kids are adults...** (Good manners and discipline should be instilled in children when they are young). |

\<hr\>

### Vocabulary

| Kikuyu Word | English Meaning |
|:---|:---|
| **Mũtĩ** | tree |
| **Kũrũnga** | to correct |
| **Kũrũnga** | make straight |
| **Mũnini** | small, young |
| **Taara** | give advice |

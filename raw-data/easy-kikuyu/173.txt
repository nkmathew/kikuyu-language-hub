## 2. Grammar and Phrases

### A. Personal Pronouns & Greetings

| Kikuyu Phrase | English Translation | Grammar Note |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Nũũ ?** | Who is it? | Uses the interrogative pronoun **Nũũ** (who). |
| **Nĩ nĩĩ** | It is me | Uses the personal pronoun **Nĩĩ** (me/I). |
| **Nĩ we ũ ?** | Who are you? | Uses the personal pronoun **we** (you). |
| **Wee wĩ mũgeni** | You are a visitor | Uses the pronoun **Wee** (you). |
| **Ĩĩ ndĩ mũgeni** | Yes I am a visitor | Uses the pronoun **ndĩ** (I am). |
| **Njitagwo Njeri** | My name is Njeri | |
| **Njeri wa ũ ?** | Whose Njeri (who is your father)? | Traditional inquiry into lineage. |
| **Thĩĩ na wega** | Go in peace | Common farewell. |

### B. Asking and Giving Instructions (Future Tense/Commands)

| Kikuyu Question/Instruction | English Translation |
|:---|:---|
| **Nĩ ũkũgũra ngũkũ?** | Will you buy a chicken? |
| **Gũra ngũkũ tũrĩe** | Buy a chicken (so) we eat |
| **Gũĩthia iembe rĩmwe** | Drop one mango |
| **Hinga ndirica ciothe** | Close all the windows |
| **Iga matumbĩ mau haha** | Put those eggs here |
| **Nyĩta haha ndĩnie kanyama** | Hold here so I cut (some) meat |
| **Ringa mũbira na hinya** | Hit the ball hard |
| **Ruga irio tũrĩe** | Cook food (so) we eat |

## 3. Proverbs and Stories

### A. Proverb on Saving

| Kikuyu Proverb | English Translation | Vocabulary |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Wĩĩgĩire ndahũtaga** | **He/she that has saved something does not starve** | **Kũĩga** (to keep, store), **Ng'aragu** (Hunger), **Kũhũta** (to be hungry) |
| **Lesson** | Make a habit of saving for hard times. | |

### B. Short Story Excerpt (Karũgano)

The excerpt introduces a story titled **Wambũĩ Mĩcore na Thigiriri (Zebra and Black Ant):**

* **ciarĩ ũrata mũno** (Were great friends)
* **Wambũĩ nĩ yatumaga rũgendo rũraihu gũthiĩ gũcererera Thigiriri** (Zebra would travel on a long journey to visit Black Ant)
* **No Thigiriri yarĩ nini mũno ya kũhota gũceragĩra Wambũĩ Mĩcore kaĩngĩ** (But Black Ant was very small to be able to visit Zebra often)
